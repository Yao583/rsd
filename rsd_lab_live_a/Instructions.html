{% trans "Step 3: Allocation Process" %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} In Step 3, the allocation process allocates the prizes to participants. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %}These details are important to learn{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} You may be able to apply your knowledge of them to make better decisions in the upcoming study. deciding how many prizes you want to rank and in what order. Some details may seem confusing at first. This is quite natural! But don't worry, we will show you step-by-step examples. Things will become clearer along the way. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} First, the process aims to match participants with prizes based on their rankings. The first part of the allocation process works in the following way: {% endblocktrans %}

  • Round 1: The participant with the first prize priority goes first. That participant will be assigned the prize they submitted first in their ranking. If this participant ranked no prizes, they remain unmatched and receive no prize.
  • If a prize was assigned to the participant with the first prize priority, that prize is excluded from the set of available prizes.
  • Round 2: The participant with the second prize priority goes second. That participant will be assigned the top prize according to the ranking they submitted among the set of available prizes. If the participant has not ranked any of the available prizes, they remain unmatched and receive no prize.
  • The set of available prizes is adjusted to exclude any prizes assigned to participants in Rounds 1 and 2.
  • And so on.
  • In general, in Round X: The participant with the Xth prize priority goes Xth. That participant will be assigned the top prize according to the ranking they submitted among the set of available prizes. If the participant has not ranked any of the available prizes, they remain unmatched and receive no prize.

{% blocktrans trimmed %} At the end of this first part of the allocation process, some participants have received prizes and some participants may be unmatched. Likewise, some prizes were allocated to participants, and some prizes may remain available prizes. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} Every unmatched participant, and only those, participate in the second allocation process, and all prizes that are still available, and only those, participate in the second allocation process. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} The second part of the allocation process works as follows. Every participant who is unmatched randomly receives one of the prizes that are still available. In this second allocation process, your ranking of prizes is not taken into account. At the end of the second allocation process, every previously unmatched participant is matched to a prize, whether they ranked it or not. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed %} At the end of Step 3, that is after the first and the second part of the allocation process, every participant is therefore matched to a prize which determines their earnings. {% endblocktrans %}
